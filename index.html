About Me I have a passion for developing applications, solving technical problems, and exploring new technologies.

Get to know me!

My journey began in Kinesiology and Health Science, captivated by how the human body works. At York University, I pursued this passion, driven by a desire to help others through science. I engaged in various extracurriculars, from tutoring and strengthening the Somali community as VP of the Somali Student Organization to enhancing student life as a Student Service Advisor. These experiences taught me that helping others extends beyond healthcare.

Near the end of my degree, I discovered coding, which quickly grew from a hobby into a passion. Building software satisfied the same problem-solving urge that drew me to health science. After graduating cum laude, I chose to further explore this interest. Now, as a second-year Computer Science student, I thrive on creating innovative software solutions that make a real difference, whether in health, tech, or beyond. My journey from health science to software development reflects my curiosity and drive to solve meaningful problems.

IncidentTrack

Developed a full-stack incident reporting system that streamlines communication between security officers and property managers. Officers can create, view, and update reports, which are automatically summarized with OpenAI and emailed to property managers.
